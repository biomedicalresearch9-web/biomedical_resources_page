Section 3
Career exploration
Neuroscience spans clinical neurology, psychiatry, cognitive science, AI research, and biotech — one of the most interdisciplinary fields in biomedicine.
Clinical careers
  • Neurologist
    Diagnoses and treats disorders of the nervous system
  • Neurosurgeon
    Performs surgery on the brain, spine, and peripheral nerves
  • Psychiatrist
    Treats mental illness with biological and psychological approaches
  • Neuropsychologist
    Assesses cognitive function and brain-behavior relationships
  • Epileptologist
    Sub-specializes in seizure disorders
  • Neuro-oncologist
    Treats brain and spinal cord tumors
Research & science careers
  • Neuroscientist / Research Scientist
    Studies brain circuits, cells, and behavior
  • Computational Neuroscientist
    Models brain function using math and machine learning
  • Neuroimaging Scientist
    Analyzes fMRI, EEG, and connectome data
  • Neurogenetics Researcher
    Studies genetic basis of neurological disorders
  • Pharmaceutical Neuroscientist
    Develops drugs for Alzheimer's, depression, Parkinson's
  • Neuroethicist
    Examines ethical implications of neurotechnology
Education pathway
Bachelor's (4 yrs)→ MD or PhD (4 yrs)→ Residency (4–7 yrs)→ Fellowship (1–2 yrs)
Clinical track: Neurology residency is 4 years (PGY1 internship + 3 yrs neurology). Neurosurgery is 7 years. Sub-specialties (epilepsy, neuro-oncology) add a fellowship.
Research track: PhD in neuroscience, biology, or psychology, followed by a postdoctoral fellowship. Computational track may include computer science or applied math.
