Section 3
Career exploration
Epidemiologists work across government agencies, academia, global health organizations, and the private sector — investigating disease patterns and shaping public health policy.
Applied & public health careers
  • Epidemiologist (CDC / State Health Dept.)
    Conducts disease surveillance and outbreak response
  • Public Health Officer
    Implements and evaluates population health programs
  • Infectious Disease Epidemiologist
    Tracks and controls communicable disease outbreaks
  • Global Health Epidemiologist
    Works with WHO, NGOs, and governments on international disease
  • Environmental Epidemiologist
    Studies how environment and toxins affect population health
  • Health Policy Analyst
    Uses epi data to shape legislation and health systems
Research & academic careers
  • Academic Epidemiologist
    Conducts research and teaches at universities
  • Clinical Epidemiologist
    Designs and analyzes clinical trials and observational studies
  • Biostatistician
    Analyzes public health data using statistical methods
  • Cancer Epidemiologist
    Studies cancer incidence, etiology, and prevention
  • Pharmacoepidemiologist
    Studies drug safety and effectiveness in populations
  • Computational Epidemiologist
    Models disease spread using mathematical and AI methods
Education pathway
Bachelor's (4 yrs)→ MPH or MS (2 yrs)→ PhD or DrPH (4–5 yrs)
Most epidemiologists hold a Master of Public Health (MPH) for applied roles, or a PhD/DrPH for research and academic careers. MD-MPH dual degrees are common for clinician-epidemiologists.
Practical experience through CDC Epidemic Intelligence Service (EIS) or FETP fellowships is highly valued.
