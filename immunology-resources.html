Section 3
Career exploration
Immunology spans clinical medicine, vaccine development, autoimmune research, and cutting-edge cell therapy — a wide range of impactful careers.
Clinical careers
  • Clinical Immunologist / Allergist
    Diagnoses and treats immune system disorders and allergies
  • Rheumatologist
    Manages autoimmune diseases: lupus, RA, MS
  • Infectious Disease Specialist
    Treats infections and immune responses to pathogens
  • Transplant Immunologist
    Manages immune rejection in organ transplant patients
  • Oncology Immunologist
    Applies immunotherapy in cancer treatment
  • Pediatric Immunologist
    Treats immune deficiencies in children
Research & science careers
  • Immunologist / Research Scientist
    Investigates immune mechanisms in disease and health
  • Vaccine Researcher
    Designs and tests vaccines for infectious and chronic diseases
  • Autoimmune Disease Researcher
    Studies breakdown of immune self-tolerance
  • CAR-T / Cell Therapy Scientist
    Engineers immune cells as therapeutic agents
  • Bioinformatician (Immunogenomics)
    Analyzes immune cell sequencing and TCR/BCR data
  • Pharmaceutical / Biotech Immunologist
    Develops biologics, antibodies, and immune modulators
Education pathway
Bachelor's (4 yrs)→ MD or PhD (4 yrs)→ Residency (3 yrs)→ Immunology Fellowship (2–3 yrs)
Clinical track: Internal Medicine or Pediatrics residency, then allergy/immunology fellowship (2 yrs). Rheumatology adds its own fellowship.
Research track: PhD in immunology, microbiology, or cell biology, followed by a postdoctoral fellowship. Many academic immunologists hold MD/PhD dual degrees.
