Section 3
Career exploration
From clinical practice to research and public health — cardiology offers a wide range of career directions.
Clinical careers
  • Medical Cardiologist
    Diagnoses and treats heart disease non-surgically
  • Interventional Cardiologist
    Performs catheterizations, stents, angioplasty
  • Electrophysiologist
    Specializes in heart rhythm and arrhythmia
  • Cardiovascular Surgeon
    Open-heart surgery, bypass, valve repair
  • Cardiac Nurse Practitioner
    Advanced practice in cardiac care settings
  • Cardiac Sonographer
    Performs echocardiograms and imaging studies
Research & science careers
  • Cardiovascular Researcher
    Investigates disease mechanisms and treatments
  • Physician-Scientist (MD/PhD)
    Bridges clinical practice and lab research
  • Biomedical Engineer
    Develops cardiac devices, stents, pacemakers
  • Cardiovascular Epidemiologist
    Studies population-level heart disease patterns
  • Pharmacologist
    Develops and tests cardiac drugs and therapies
  • Bioinformatician
    Analyzes genomic and clinical cardiac datasets
Education pathway
Bachelor's (4 yrs)→ MD or PhD (4 yrs)→ Residency (3 yrs)→ Cardiology Fellowship (3 yrs)
Clinical track: Internal Medicine residency followed by a 3-year cardiology fellowship. Sub-specialty fellowships add 1–2 more years.
Research track: MD/PhD programs or a postdoctoral fellowship after PhD.
